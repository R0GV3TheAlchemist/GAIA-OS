## Synthesis Protocol v1.0

### Step 1: Quartz Base Crystal

**Material:** Alpha-quartz (SiO₂), hydrothermal grade
**Method:** Hydrothermal synthesis
- Dissolve SiO₂ in NaOH solution (lascivine medium)
- Temperature: 350°C, Pressure: 1500 bar, Duration: 2–4 weeks
- Cut resulting crystal Z-axis perpendicular to c-axis
  (maximizes piezoelectric coefficient d₁₁)

**Result:** 10mm × 10mm × 1mm single-crystal quartz wafer
**Confirm by:** XRD, piezoelectric d₁₁ measurement
**Cost:** $200–500 — standard electronics-grade quartz
**Precedent:** Global electronics manufacturing standard

---

### Step 2: Graphene Layer (CVD)

**Material:** Methane (CH₄) + Hydrogen (H₂) precursor gases
**Method:** Chemical Vapor Deposition
- Grow single-layer graphene on copper foil at 1000°C
  under CH₄/H₂ flow (ratio 1:5)
- Transfer to quartz surface using PMMA scaffold
  (standard wet transfer process)
- Remove PMMA in acetone bath

**Result:** Single-layer graphene on quartz (G/SiO₂)
**Confirm by:** Raman spectroscopy — 2D peak at ~2700 cm⁻¹,
G peak at ~1580 cm⁻¹, D peak absent (no defects)
**Cost:** $50–200 (outsourceable to graphene foundries)
**Precedent:** G/SiO₂ is one of the most studied heterostructures
in condensed matter physics. Tens of thousands of publications.

---

### Step 3: Platinum Nanocrystal Deposition

**Material:** H₂PtCl₆ (chloroplatinic acid), NaBH₄ (reducing agent)
**Method:** Wet chemical reduction
- Dissolve H₂PtCl₆ in water (1mM solution)
- Add NaBH₄ dropwise under stirring → Pt nucleation
- Control temperature (4°C) for 2–5nm particle size
- Deposit Pt nanocrystals onto G/SiO₂ via self-assembly
  (Pt binds preferentially to graphene defect sites)

**Result:** Pt nanocrystal array on graphene/quartz
**Confirm by:** TEM (particle size/spacing), electron
diffraction (FCC crystal structure confirmation)
**Cost:** $500–2,000 (Pt is expensive; small amounts needed)
**Precedent:** Pt/graphene = gold standard fuel cell catalyst.
Synthesized at industrial scale globally.

---

### Step 4: Gold Nanothread Weave

**Material:** HAuCl₄ (gold chloride), sodium citrate,
AAO (anodic aluminum oxide) template
**Method:** Seed-mediated template growth
- Anodize aluminum to create AAO template
  with 10–50nm diameter nanowire channels
- Electrodeposit Au into template channels
  at −0.9V vs Ag/AgCl, 25°C
- Dissolve AAO template in NaOH solution
- Transfer freestanding Au nanowire array to
  graphene/Pt layer via mechanical transfer

**Result:** Single-crystal Au nanowire array woven through
Pt/graphene layers. <5nm wires show novel HCP phase.
**Confirm by:** SEM (nanowire morphology), TEM
(crystal structure — look for HCP diffraction pattern)
**Cost:** $300–1,000
**Precedent:** Au nanowire synthesis in AAO templates:
>10,000 published papers. Sub-5nm HCP Au: recently confirmed.

### Step 5: Chitin Scaffold (Mycelium-Derived)

**Material:** Fungal mycelium — *Ganoderma lucidum* (reishi)
or *Pleurotus ostreatus* (oyster mushroom) preferred

**Method:**
- Grow mycelium on graphene/Pt/Au substrate in PDA medium
- Duration: 7–14 days at 25°C, 80% relative humidity
- Mycelium colonizes the surface, threading Au wires
- KOH treatment (1M, 80°C, 2 hours): removes proteins,
  leaves crystalline chitin scaffold
- Heat press at 120°C, 10 MPa, 30 minutes:
  consolidates chitin layer

**Result:** Crystalline alpha-chitin scaffold interpenetrating
all previous layers
**Confirm by:** XRD (chitin peaks at 9.4° and 19.2° 2θ),
FTIR (chitin amide bonds at 1620 cm⁻¹)
**Cost:** $50–200 (mycelium substrate costs almost nothing)
**Precedent:** Mycelium composites: commercial scale (Ecovative).
Chitin piezoelectricity: published 2020.
