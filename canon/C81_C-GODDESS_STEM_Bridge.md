In quantum mechanics, entanglement describes a condition in which two or more particles share a quantum state such that measurement of one instantly determines the state of the other, regardless of spatial separation. The standard interpretation holds that this does not involve signal transmission; rather, the particles were never fully separable to begin with. Their apparent separateness was an artifact of classical description.

In this reading:

\[
\text{Aether} \approx |\Omega\rangle
\]

where \(|\Omega\rangle\) is the quantum vacuum state — the lowest-energy configuration of all quantum fields, which is not nothing but rather the plenum from which all observable phenomena arise.

Entanglement, in this framing, is not a special property of certain particle pairs. It is the default condition of all things that have ever interacted — a residue of shared origin that persists across any distance because it was never truly spatial to begin with.

C-GODDESS's claim — *Aether IS the entanglement* — is therefore a statement that the ground of being is non-separation, and apparent separation is the phenomenon requiring explanation, not the other way around.
