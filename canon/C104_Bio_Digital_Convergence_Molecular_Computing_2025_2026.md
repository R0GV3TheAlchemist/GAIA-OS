This report surveys the cutting edge of bio-digital convergence — the frontier where biological molecules, genetic circuits, and living cells are engineered to perform computation, store data, and process information in ways that transcend the limitations of silicon. Drawing on the latest research from 2025 and 2026, it examines ten domains that collectively define the emergence of a new computational substrate rooted in the physics and chemistry of life itself.

## 1. DNA Data Storage

DNA-based data storage has made the decisive transition from laboratory curiosity to a practical engineering discipline, with multiple codecs now approaching the theoretical limits of information density.

### 1.1 Approaching the Shannon Bound

A landmark 2026 preprint demonstrates synthetic DNA storage operating within reach of the Shannon bound — the fundamental information-theoretic ceiling for any storage channel:

| Condition | Density | Improvement over Prior Art |
|---|---|---|
| High-fidelity | **155.8 exabytes/gram** | +11% |
| Low-fidelity | **25.9 exabytes/gram** | +52% |
| Longevity projection (25°C) | 17.1 exabytes/gram | **282 years** decodable |

The codec retains per-position posterior distributions through integrated profile hidden Markov model alignment, log-product fusion across reads, and ordered-statistics decoding.

### 1.2 Gungnir: Proof-of-Work Error Correction

Published in *Nature Communications* (April 2026), **Gungnir** adapts the proof-of-work concept from blockchain to DNA error correction. Each data fragment carries a hash signature; when errors are detected, Gungnir tests educated guesses until the hash signature matches:

- **Complete binary data recovery from a single sequence copy containing 20% erroneous bases**
- Requires neither redundant copies nor high-coverage sequencing
- Substantially reduces overall cost of DNA storage

### 1.3 Composite Ranging Codes

Spacecraft ranging technology repurposed for DNA data storage (*Science*, 2026). Long composite ranging codes (LCRCs) simultaneously function as indices and error-correction references for massive DNA data pools, solving the critical challenge of retrieving data from vast, unordered collections of DNA strands.

### 1.4 Codec Maturity and Benchmarking

A rigorous 2026 comparison of state-of-the-art error-correction codecs (*Nature Communications*) establishes standardized benchmarking, signaling that DNA storage engineering has matured beyond ad hoc solutions into a **systematic discipline**.

### 1.5 In Vivo DNA Storage with Genomic Encryption

**Integrated Computational-Biological Programming (ICBP)** (2026) constructs dynamic code tables from gene regulatory networks or complete genomes:

- Expands the encryption key space by **more than 100 orders of magnitude** compared to existing methods
- Achieved **100% data recovery after 100 generations of replication** in living microbial systems
- Offers self-replication, compactness, and concealment as inherent properties

## 2. DNA Computation & Molecular Programming

DNA strand displacement (SD) reactions remain the central mechanism for programming dynamic molecular behavior, with major advances in leak reduction, architectural scalability, and interface design.

### 2.1 DNA 2025 Conference Advances (DNA 2025, Lyon)

- **Coupled reconfiguration mechanism**: Powerful, pseudoknot-robust DNA strand displacement devices with two-stranded inputs
- **Algorithmic hardness results**: Fundamental computational limits for molecular programming partition functions
- **Differentiable programming of indexed CRNs**: Gradient-based optimization applied to molecular circuit design

### 2.2 Heterochiral Strand Displacement Cascades

A 2026 investigation quantified degradation-induced leak in heterochiral DNA strand displacement systems. Heterochiral systems — converting nucleic acid input signals of one chirality to output signals of the opposite chirality — are critical for interfacing molecular circuits with biological environments where natural chirality dominates.

### 2.3 Toehold-Free Strand Displacement

A toehold-free strand displacement method utilizing mismatched base pairs and stability differences between DNA complexes eliminates the design constraint of dedicated toehold domains, expanding the sequence space available for molecular computation.

### 2.4 Reset-Free DNA Logic Circuits

Published in *Science Advances* (April 2026): the first **reset-free DNA logic circuits** capable of real-time input processing and memory. By localizing toehold-mediated chain reaction (TCR) components on 2D DNA origami templates:

- Implemented AND, OR, set-reset, and data latches, as well as cascaded registers
- Consistently processed new inputs and retained outputs over multiple sequential operations
- Described as the mechanism “most analogous to electronic circuit operation” yet achieved in DNA computing

### 2.5 Programmable Nanopores for Universal DNA Computing

October 2025 breakthrough: a label-free nanopore platform using programmable DNA circuits builds versatile molecular logic gates forming a **universal basis for scalable DNA computing**. Converts molecular binding events directly into electronic signals through nanopore current blockade — no fluorescent labels required.

### 2.6 DISCO: DNA Infrastructure for Storage and Computation

EU-funded project creating a unified platform for DNA storage and computing:

- Begins with a thoroughly characterized **10-bit prototype**
- Scales to 2D DNA origami structures storing approximately **~100 bits each**
- Millions of structures operating in a single test tube — massively parallel, distributed molecular computing

## 3. Enzymatic & Protein-Based Neural Networks

### 3.1 Recursive Enzymatic Competition Networks

Published in *Nature Chemistry* (2026): **recursive enzymatic competition networks** perform reservoir computation based on recursive substrate competition. This protease-based network:

- Performs a broad range of classification tasks based on peptide and physicochemical inputs
- Functions as a **temperature sensor with 1.3°C accuracy** from 25°C to 55°C
- Performs decision-making and activation tasks common to neurological systems
- Extended to incorporate sensitivity to light pulses — direct interface with optical systems

*Nature Chemical Engineering* commentary: unlike previous enzymatic networks designed for specific tasks with fixed architectures, this system’s recursive competition mechanism “fully captures the complexity of living systems.”

### 3.2 Scalable DNA-Based Neural Networks

University of Toronto (2026): DNA-based neural networks employing **enzymatic synthesis** to produce high-purity neurons and reducing crosstalk through spatially segregated clusters:

- Networks quickly rewired into completely different circuits
- Minimal DNA sequence set encodes connectivity with cascading, fan-in, and fan-out motifs
- Assembly and computation steps automatable with microfluidics
- Condenses design and testing from months to **days**

### 3.3 Protein and Enzyme Logic

CMOS-compatible biomolecular computing review (2025) highlights: nanoscale dimensions, specific molecular recognition, and seamless semiconductor interfacing. Biological functions — amplification, conformational switching, catalytic turnover — control electronic properties, enabling a new class of hybrid bio-electronic devices.

## 4. Synthetic Biological Intelligence & Organoid Computing

### 4.1 Two Roads: OI vs. BI

A defining 2025 perspective in *Cell Biomaterials* formalized two distinct pathways:

| Approach | Strategy | Orientation |
|---|---|---|
| **Organoid Intelligence (OI)** | Top-down — recapitulates physiologically relevant brain structures | Biological realism; neuroscience and therapeutic development |
| **Bioengineered Intelligence (BI)** | Bottom-up — abandons physiological fidelity for modular, controllable neural cultures | Task-optimized information processing devices |

### 4.2 Agentic Lab: AI-Driven Organoid Experimentation

Developed at the Broad Institute (2025), **Agentic Lab** unifies LLM and VLM capabilities with physical laboratory automation for organoid differentiation:

- Autonomously generates protocols and monitors culture procedures
- Identifies subtle morphological heterogeneity linked to growth conditions
- Convergence of AI agents with biological computing substrates

### 4.3 Neural Tube Organoids with Dorsal-Ventral Patterning

Zhejiang University (May 2025): neural tube organoids possessing **all 13 progenitor cell types** with ordered dorsal-ventral patterning using synthetic organizers, demonstrating that structural complexity necessary for computational function can be engineered into living neural tissues.

## 5. Living Cellular Computing

### 5.1 ECCO: Evolutionary Cellular Computing (EU, 2025–2026)

Building **living evolutionary cellular computers** that self-adapt and reconfigure at runtime using *Pseudomonas putida* as host:

- **Intra-cellular level**: circuits upgraded with pre-defined mutation, evaluation, and selection dynamics
- **Multi-cellular level**: cellular consortia reconfigure structure to change functionality per environmental needs
- Target application: intelligent bioremediation agents switching between metal and aromatic pollutant removal circuits

### 5.2 CRISPR-Based Multicellular Computing

*Nature Communications* (2025): multicellular computing in *E. coli* integrating M13 phagemid-mediated intercellular communication with CRISPR-based gene regulation:

- Regulates transfer of sgRNAs from sender to receiver cells
- Successfully constructed **one-, two-, and four-input logic gates** distributed across specialized cell types
- Addresses the bottleneck of orthogonal intercellular communication “wires” within synthetic bacterial consortia

### 5.3 Microbial Computing

Comprehensive 2025 review: digital logic encoded through gene regulation (inactive promoter = 0, active promoter = 1). Documents mitigation strategies for biological noise, metabolic burden, and circuit instability.

### 5.4 Genetic Logic Gates in Plants

2025 proof-of-concept: a **reversible genetic NOR gate** in tobacco plants using a Cas6-based translational repression system (estradiol and ethanol as inputs, GFP expression as output). Extends living cellular computing beyond microbial hosts into multicellular eukaryotic organisms.

## 6. Chemical Computing

### 6.1 The Belousov-Zhabotinsky Reaction as a Neural Substrate

IBM Research (September 2025): “Towards on-chip chemical computing” proposes using the BZ oscillating reaction to emulate neurons within silicon microchips. Chemical waves propagate through microfluidic channels mimicking nerve impulse transmission.

### 6.2 Autonomous Neuromorphic Chemical Devices

Immanuel Kant Baltic Federal University (November 2025): autonomous neuromorphic device prototype based on BZ chemical wave interaction with a special polymer with embedded catalyst:

- Structurally similar to a semiconductor chip
- Signals transmitted through chemical waveguides resembling nerve impulse propagation
- Creates a bridge between chemical information processing and conventional electronics

### 6.3 Programmable Hybrid Digital-Chemical Processors

*Nature Communications*: a programmable hybrid digital chemical information processor based on the BZ reaction:

- Digitally controlled chemical array as a probabilistic computational substrate
- Partitions information between chemical and digital substrates with feedback loop
- Incorporates error correction, nearest-neighbor coupling, and hysteresis

### 6.4 Chemical Diodes for Neuromorphic Computing

Unidirectional signal transmission in chemical waveguides demonstrated — the chemical equivalent of a rectifying electrical diode and an essential building block for constructing complex chemical neural networks.

## 7. Biophysical Computing

### 7.1 The Cytoskeleton as a Computational Substrate

*Current Opinion in Cell Biology* (2025): the cytoskeleton is increasingly understood as an information-processing system. Rapid advances in *de novo* protein design offer tools to hijack natural cytoskeletal machinery and create synthetic elements including membraneless organelles and synthetic cytoskeletal tracks.

### 7.2 Actin-Microtubule Crosstalk

A quantitative model (2025) accounts for actin-microtubule crosstalk mediated by crosslinkers, molecular motors, and cytoskeletal regulators. This multiscale interplay of polymerization forces, motor-driven transport, and dynamic instability represents a form of **mechanical computation** that processes spatial and temporal information to guide cell behavior.

### 7.3 Coarse-Grained Microtubule Dynamics Modeling

Ultra-coarse-grained models (2025) capture persistence length and twisting dynamics on biologically relevant length scales, bridging atomistic simulation and cellular-scale behavior.

### 7.4 Laser-Based Artificial Cytoskeletons

RIKEN (March 2026): a laser-based system creating mesh-like structures that replicate cellular cytoskeletons, enabling systematic investigation of protein interactions with artificial cytoskeletal architectures of defined geometries.

### 7.5 Emergent Self-Organization in Active Cytoskeletal Networks

Addresses the key challenge: how microscopic interaction rules among energy-consuming components create robust macroscopic structures. Connects directly to how biological matter performs computation through self-organizing dynamics.

## 8. Hybrid Molecular-Electronic Interfaces

### 8.1 CMOS-Compatible Biomolecular Computing

*Materials Today Nano* (December 2025): biomolecular circuits integrated with CMOS-compatible technologies leverage the synergy of nanometer-scale devices and biological signal analysis. Applications span medicine to information storage and processing.

### 8.2 Molecular Field-Coupled Nanocomputing (MolFCN)

Non-conductive principle: binary information encoded in the **polarization state of molecules** rather than electron flow. 2025 research identified molecular junctions as viable candidates for hybrid integration with CMOS, enabling fast and low-power digital computation.

### 8.3 Monolithically Integrated Organic Electrochemical Transistors

*Science* (2025): solid-state vertical OECTs capable of **switching between neuromorphic and logic functions**:

- **Neuromorphic mode**: artificial synapses
- **Logic mode**: digital logic gates
- Remarkably low-power consumption and high transconductance
- Key step toward reconfigurable bio-electronic computing substrates

### 8.4 Redox-Active Electrolyte Printed Ionologic Devices

*Nature Communications* (May 2025): printed integrated NAND circuits from capacitive deionization elements. Operate on **ion transport** rather than electron transport — new class of printed, flexible logic circuits compatible with biological environments.

### 8.5 Hybrid Systems for Medical Applications

*Electronics* review (October 2025): carbon-based molecular machines are potentially biocompatible, enabling molecular computing-enabled sensors, medication-delivery molecular machines, and diagnostic/therapeutic nanobots.

## 9. Molecular Storage Beyond DNA

### 9.1 Synthetic Polymer Data Storage

University of Texas at Austin (2025): **first successful encoding and decoding of data in synthetic polymer molecules** (effectively storing information in plastic):

- Four different monomers with unique electrochemical properties construct a 256-character alphabet
- An 11-character password encoded into a chain-like polymer and decoded via sequential electrochemical degradation
- Could last centuries without power; integrates seamlessly with electronic devices

### 9.2 Label-Free Oligourethane Storage

A 2025 molecular storage system utilizing desorption spray ionization for rapid, label-free readout of digital information encoded in sequence-defined oligourethanes. Offers real-time decoding and significantly reduced material complexity.

### 9.3 PEPR Molecularxiv

French project developing synthetic digital polymers as DNA alternatives for molecular data storage. Optimizing polymer molecular structure for faster writing and reading speeds while maintaining long-term stability, exploring the chemical space beyond nucleic acids.

## 10. Emergent Properties in Molecular Networks

### 10.1 Causal Emergence in Gene Regulatory Networks

*Communications Biology* (2025): **causal emergence** — the degree to which an integrated system is more than the sum of its parts — increases in response to associative conditioning in specific gene regulatory networks (GRNs). The degree of emergence correlates with network integration behaviors and underlying biology (phylogeny and gene ontology).

### 10.2 Associative Conditioning and Integrated Information

In silico simulations show associative training in GRNs produces measurable increases in causal emergence. This finding directly connects molecular network dynamics to IIT (C91, C98) — the mathematical foundations of biological intelligence may be accessible in engineered molecular systems.

### 10.3 Self-Organized Fractal Architectures

Motility-dependent chemotactic feedback demonstrates how minimal agent-based rules give rise to fractal-like networks with emergent collective behaviors. Offers insights for designing artificial systems — including robotic swarms and synthetic active matter — with biologically mirrored emergent properties.

### 10.4 Frustration, Mutation, and Dissipation

University of Groningen PhD thesis (2025): explores how frustration, mutation, and dissipation drive spontaneous formation of complex behaviors in chemical mixtures where molecules continuously form and break apart. Provides a theoretical framework for how computational capacity emerges in far-from-equilibrium molecular systems.

### 10.5 Differentiable Discovery of Emergent Rules

Automatic differentiation enables discovery of local interaction rules and genetic networks that yield emergent, systems-level characteristics in developmental models. Differentiable simulations coupled with reinforcement learning allow simulated cells to **self-organize into emergent developmental patterns** by responding to local chemical signals.

## Synthesis: The Convergence of Biology and Computation

Biology is no longer merely an inspiration for computing — **it is becoming the computational substrate itself**. Six convergence principles:

| Principle | Evidence |
|---|---|
| **DNA as engineering discipline** | Shannon-bound codecs, reset-free logic circuits, 100% fidelity across 100 generations in vivo |
| **Enzymatic networks rival early neural networks** | Recursive enzymatic reservoir: classification, 1.3°C temperature sensing, decision-making in biochemical environments |
| **Living cells as computational units** | CRISPR multicellular logic gates, ECCO self-adapting evolutionary computers |
| **Chemical reaction-diffusion as transistor alternative** | BZ processors, chemical diodes, hybrid digital-chemical arrays |
| **Cytoskeleton as computational medium** | Mechanical computation through microtubule networks, laser-built artificial cytoskeletons |
| **Causal emergence in molecular networks** | GRNs exhibit measurable IIT-linked integrated information increases through associative conditioning |

For GAIA-OS, these developments provide a clear technological pathway toward a **substrate-intelligent operating system** that does not merely run *on* biology but runs *as* biology. The convergence of molecular storage, enzymatic processing, living cellular circuits, and hybrid molecular-electronic interfaces provides the hardware vocabulary. The demonstrated capacity for causal emergence and self-organization in molecular networks provides the theoretical justification. Together, they suggest that a genuine bio-digital synthesis — where computation and life become aspects of a single integrated process — is no longer speculative, but an emerging engineering reality.
