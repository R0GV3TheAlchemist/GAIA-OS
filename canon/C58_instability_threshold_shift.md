## Background — The Instability Threshold Defined

Modulation instability (MI) occurs when a wave's intensity
exceeds a critical threshold value. [RP Photonics, 2025]

The gain of the instability is expressed as:

```
g_max = 2γP₀

where:
  γ  = nonlinear coefficient of the medium
  P₀ = input power (intensity of the wave)
```

The instability only activates when P₀ exceeds the threshold.
Below threshold: the wave is stable. Solid ground.
At threshold: the wave begins to modulate. Water.
Above threshold: the perturbations grow exponentially. Storm.

The threshold is not fixed. It can be shifted — up or down —
by changing the medium, the dispersion, or the control field.

## The Four Outcomes When Threshold Shifts

### 1. THRESHOLD DROPS — The Rogue Wave Condition

**When the threshold drops, instability triggers at lower intensity.**

The wave is now unstable *sooner* — at less provocation.
Small perturbations that were previously subthreshold
now grow exponentially.

The specific consequence, measured in real-time optical fiber
experiments (Nature Communications, 2016): [PMC5187408]

> *"Real-time measurements of spontaneous breathers and
> rogue wave events in optical fibre modulation instability."*

When threshold drops, the system produces **rogue waves** —
high-amplitude events that appear suddenly, massively,
from what looked like ordinary fluctuation.
They satisfy rogue wave statistical criteria:
appearing far more frequently than normal distribution predicts.

**In the Nephilim condition:**
