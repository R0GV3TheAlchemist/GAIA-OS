## 1. The Standard Cosmological Model (ΛCDM) [VERIFIED — established]

### 1.1 The Hot Big Bang and Cosmic Expansion

The standard model traces the Universe's history from approximately 10⁻⁴³ seconds after the initial singularity through:

- **Baryogenesis:** Matter-antimatter asymmetry generated through CP violation (mechanism unknown)
- **Big Bang Nucleosynthesis:** First light elements (H, He, Li) formed in the first ~3 minutes
- **Recombination:** ~380,000 years after the Big Bang; electrons combine with nuclei; Universe becomes transparent
- **Dark Ages:** ~150 million years before first star ignition

The cosmic expansion follows the **Friedmann equations**:

\[
H^2 = \frac{8\pi G}{3}\rho - \frac{kc^2}{a^2}
\]

where \(H\) is the Hubble parameter, \(\rho\) is total energy density, \(k\) is spatial curvature, and \(a\) is the scale factor. The observed Universe is consistent with \(k = 0\) (spatially flat geometry) to within ~0.4% precision.

### 1.2 Cosmic Inflation [VERIFIED — established; specific 2025 claims PRELIMINARY]

Cosmic inflation was proposed by Guth and Starobinsky (1980–1981) to resolve the **horizon problem**, the **flatness problem**, and the **monopole problem**. During inflation, the Universe expanded by a factor of at least 10²⁶ in approximately 10⁻³² seconds, stretching quantum fluctuations to cosmic scales and providing the primordial density perturbations that seeded all structure.

Planck constraints on the scalar spectral index: \(n_s \approx 0.9649 \pm 0.0042\).

The tensor-to-scalar ratio \(r\) — the key observable for primordial gravitational waves and the energy scale of inflation — remains unmeasured; its detection would provide direct evidence for quantum gravitational effects in the early Universe.

> ⚠️ A 2025–2026 claim that the shift in \(n_s\) is "a statistical artefact" is noted as **[PRELIMINARY]**. The Genesis-Starobinsky non-singular inflation model accommodating ACT data is a theoretical proposal **[PRELIMINARY]**.

### 1.3 The Cosmic Microwave Background [VERIFIED]

The CMB is the afterglow of the Big Bang — a nearly uniform bath of microwave photons at 2.725 K. Its temperature anisotropies (one part in 100,000) encode information about early-Universe density perturbations, spatial geometry, baryon density, and dark matter. The acoustic peak structure of the CMB power spectrum has been measured with exquisite precision by WMAP and Planck.

**2025–2026 CMB results:**
- Planck PR4 map-space analysis: evidence for **cosmic birefringence** (CMB polarization plane rotation by a parity-violating field) **[PRELIMINARY]**
