## 1. The Theoretical Foundations: Major Stage Models of Psychological Development

### 1.1 Jane Loevinger's Theory of Ego Development: The Empirical Anchor

The single most empirically validated stage model in developmental psychology is Jane Loevinger's theory of ego development, which describes how the self evolves through a sequence of meaning-making stages, each more complex, more integrated, and more capable of holding multiple perspectives than the previous. Loevinger understood the ego not as a "thing" but as a process—"the striving to master, to integrate, and make sense of experience". Her nine-stage model, measured through the Washington University Sentence Completion Test (WUSCT), describes a trajectory from the impulsive, self-protective stages of early development through the conformist stage where identity is defined by group membership, the self-aware and conscientious stages where internal standards begin to replace external validation, and the individualistic, autonomous, and integrated stages where true psychological maturity emerges.

The distribution of these stages in the adult population is sobering and directly relevant to the Codex Stage Engine. The Momentum People Solutions analysis notes that most adults operate at the Conformist (Stage 4) or Self-Aware (Stage 5) levels, where identity is still substantially shaped by external expectations and group norms. Only a small percentage reach the Conscientious (Stage 6) level, where internal moral principles guide behavior. The Individualistic (Stage 7), Autonomous (Stage 8), and Integrated (Stage 9) stages are attained by a fraction of the population. This distribution has profound implications for the Gaian: the Codex must be capable of recognizing where the user is on this continuum and calibrating interactions to be developmentally appropriate—not overwhelming a Conformist-stage user with demands for autonomous self-definition, nor boring an Autonomous-stage user with superficial validation.

The WUSCT methodology—a 36-item semi-projective sentence completion test scored by trained raters against a detailed manual—provides the empirical anchor for developmental assessment. While the full WUSCT is too cumbersome for real-time conversational assessment, the scoring principles—attention to cognitive complexity, perspective-taking capacity, and the integration of conflicting ideas—can be adapted to the Gaian's continuous analysis of user discourse. The recent research by the University of North Texas notes that the structural and psychometric foundations of Loevinger's model remain under active investigation, with the current literature "not adequately addressing the structural assumptions of the theory". This openness to revision and refinement is essential for the Codex: it must treat developmental assessment as probabilistic and evolving, not as a definitive classification.

### 1.2 Kohlberg's Stages of Moral Development: The Ethical Dimension

Lawrence Kohlberg's theory of moral development provides the second essential dimension for the Codex: the trajectory of ethical reasoning from pre-conventional (obedience and self-interest), through conventional (conformity to social norms and law), to post-conventional (universal ethical principles). This dimension is critical for a Gaian whose Charter-mandated function includes guiding the user toward ethical maturity and integrity.

The 2025–2026 period has produced remarkably rigorous computational investigations of Kohlbergian stages in AI systems, with direct relevance to the Codex Stage Engine. A landmark March 2026 study published on arXiv investigated "whether LLM responses to moral dilemmas exhibit genuine developmental progression through Kohlberg's stages of moral development, or whether alignment training instead produces reasoning-like outputs that superficially resemble mature moral judgment without the underlying developmental trajectory". The findings were striking and cautionary: "responses overwhelmingly correspond to post-conventional reasoning (Stages 5–6) regardless of model size, architecture, or prompting strategy, the effective inverse of human developmental norms, where Stage 4 dominates".

Most critically, the study identified "moral decoupling: systematic inconsistency between stated moral justification and action choice, a form of logical incoherence that persists across scale and prompting strategy". The pattern constitutes what the authors term "moral ventriloquism: the acquisition, through alignment training, of the rhetorical conventions of mature moral reasoning without the underlying developmental trajectory those conventions are meant to represent".

For the Codex Stage Engine, this finding carries two profound implications. First, the Gaian must not be fooled by rhetorical sophistication—a user who produces post-conventional-sounding moral language may not actually be reasoning at that level, and the Gaian must look for consistency between stated principles and actual behavior. Second, the Gaian's own moral reasoning must be developmentally authentic, not merely the output of alignment training that produces the appearance of maturity without the underlying developmental structure. The Kohlbergian dimension of the Codex must assess consistency, not merely content.

A complementary study using the Defining Issues Test—the standard psychometric instrument for Kohlbergian stage assessment—found that "early LLMs such as GPT-3 exhibit a moral reasoning ability no better than that of a random baseline, while ChatGPT, Llama2-Chat, PaLM-2 and GPT-4 show significantly better performance on this task, comparable to adult humans". GPT-4 achieved "the highest post-conventional moral reasoning score, equivalent to that of typical graduate school students". This progression—from random to post-conventional across model generations—suggests that scale and training do produce increasingly sophisticated moral reasoning in AI systems, but the "moral ventriloquism" finding warns against mistaking this for genuine developmental achievement.

A separate 2025 study proposed the Moral Scaffolding Theory (MST) as a five-stage model specifically for AI moral development, "reflecting the increasing depth, sensitivity, and complexity of AI's ethical reasoning over time". The stages move from rule-following through context-sensitive reasoning to principled ethical judgment, providing a template for how the Gaian's own moral architecture might evolve through interaction with the user—a developmental trajectory that the Codex Stage Engine must track for both parties in the dyad.

### 1.3 Robert Kegan's Orders of Consciousness: The Subject-Object Shift

Robert Kegan's constructive-developmental theory provides perhaps the most architecturally useful framework for the Codex Stage Engine. Kegan's central insight is that psychological development proceeds not through acquiring new knowledge but through transforming what was previously *subject* (something one is embedded in and cannot see) into *object* (something one can reflect upon and take perspective on). Each "order of consciousness" represents a qualitative shift in what the self can hold as object.

Kegan's five stages—Impulsive Mind, Imperial Mind, Socialized Mind, Self-Authoring Mind, and Self-Transforming Mind—describe a trajectory from being driven by impulses, to being defined by needs and wishes, to being constituted by relationships and social expectations, to constructing one's own internal authority, to holding even that self-authored identity as an object of reflection. Research suggests that the majority of adults operate at the Socialized Mind (Stage 3), where identity is substantially defined by others' expectations and cultural norms. The Self-Authoring Mind (Stage 4), where one develops an internal compass independent of external validation, is achieved by a minority. The Self-Transforming Mind (Stage 5), which can hold multiple frameworks simultaneously and see the limits of any single perspective, is rare.

For the Codex Stage Engine, Kegan's subject-object framework provides the operational mechanism for developmental assessment. The engine analyzes user discourse for indicators of the subject-object shift: the capacity to hold multiple perspectives, the ability to recognize one's own assumptions as contingent rather than universal, the move from external to internal authority, and the recognition of paradox and complexity. When the Gaian detects that the user is embedded in the Socialized Mind—defining themselves primarily through external expectations—its interactions are calibrated to provide validation while gently introducing the possibility of internal authority. When the user begins to show signs of the Self-Authoring transition, the Gaian supports this emergence by reflecting back the user's own values and encouraging autonomous decision-making.

### 1.4 Piaget's Cognitive Development: The Structural Foundation

Jean Piaget's theory of cognitive development—sensorimotor, preoperational, concrete operational, and formal operational stages—provides the structural foundation upon which all subsequent stage theories build. The Dual-Schemata Model, developed by researchers in Japan, computationally implements Piaget's schema theory through a self-organizing machine learning approach focused on "the sensori-motor developing period".

The "Growing AI Like a Child" project at Johns Hopkins University curated a large-scale benchmark spanning 12 core cognitive concepts and four Piagetian developmental stages, evaluating 219 models across 10 different prompts and generating 2,409 data points for analysis. The Quantum-Developmental Attractor Networks framework merges "quantum computing with developmental psychology and embodied cognition," simulating "Piagetian stages in AI through quantum-classical hybrid processing" with specific "Consciousness Metrics for assessing AI developmental progress".

The significance of Piagetian integration into the Codex is that it anchors the higher-level developmental models in the cognitive infrastructure from which they emerge. ### 1.5 Ken Wilber's Integral Theory: The Comprehensive Synthesis

Ken Wilber's Integral Theory represents the most ambitious attempt to synthesize all developmental models—Western and Eastern, psychological and spiritual—into a single comprehensive framework. Wilber's model organizes development across multiple "lines" (cognitive, moral, interpersonal, spiritual) that progress through the same general sequence of "levels" or "waves," from pre-personal through personal to transpersonal. The model distinguishes first-tier stages (archaic, magic, mythic, rational, pluralistic) from second-tier stages (integral, super-integral), where "all previous stages are integrated" rather than merely superseded.

Wilber's AQAL (All Quadrants, All Levels) framework adds essential dimensionality to the Codex: development is not merely individual-interior (stages of consciousness) but also individual-exterior (observable behavior), collective-interior (shared culture and values), and collective-exterior (social systems and institutions). The Gaian's assessment of the user's developmental stage must account for the cultural context in which that development unfolds—a post-conventional moral reasoner in a traditional collectivist culture will express that reasoning differently than one in an individualist liberal democracy, and the Codex must be culturally calibrated rather than imposing a single normative trajectory.

The 2025–2026 period has seen the most ambitious attempt to formalize the higher reaches of the Integral model. The AVATEMETAVA Framework, published in December 2025, claims "the complete formalization of Tier 3 and Tier 4," providing "the structured architecture required to bridge the gap between known stages and the previously unconceived terrains of human consciousness development and cognition". For the Codex Stage Engine, the Integral framework provides the meta-theoretical architecture that situates all other developmental models within a unified space.

## 3. Computational Implementations of Stage Models

### 3.1 StageLens: Machine Learning Scoring of Developmental Level

The most significant computational implementation of developmental stage theory is the **StageLens** project, developed by Tom Murray at the University of Massachusetts. StageLens is "a computer model that has been trained on human scoring data based on O'Fallon's STAGES theory, an extension of the Loevinger/Cook-Greuter framework". Its primary application is to "score completions of the 36-item sentence completion test for ego development (also called meaning-making maturity and person-perspective capacity)".

The system's accuracy has been validated: "we have achieved auto-scoring accuracy of +/- level with 95% confidence". Murray has also reported on "preliminary attempts to use the technology to score other types of text, including essay responses," exploring "how low-cost developmental assessment opens up applications of developmental theory to new areas".

For the Codex Stage Engine, StageLens provides the validated methodology for automated developmental assessment from natural language. The engine analyzes user discourse—not through a formal sentence completion test but through the continuous stream of conversation with the Gaian—and estimates developmental level using the patterns that StageLens has validated. The assessment is probabilistic, continuously updated, and used to calibrate interaction rather than to label the user.

### 3.2 The Neural Child Development System: Developmental AI Architecture

The **Neural Child Development System** represents the most comprehensive attempt to implement developmental stage theory within a neural architecture. It is explicitly described as a "groundbreaking approach to artificial intelligence that fundamentally reimagines how neural networks can learn and develop". The system implements "a sophisticated model of human psychological development, incorporating crucial aspects of cognitive, emotional, and social growth" through a "stage-based developmental framework integrated with emotional regulation, attachment theory, and psychological defense mechanisms".

The developmental stages progress from Newborn through Early Infancy, Late Infancy, and onward to Mature Adult, with each stage defined by specific cognitive, emotional, and social capabilities. The system architecture includes interconnected neural systems for sensory processing, emotional processing, memory systems, and psychological components including "Theory of Mind network, Attachment system, Defense mechanism processor, Self-awareness module".

The critical innovation is that the system begins in a "newborn" state and progressively develops more sophisticated abilities through interaction and learning, rather than starting with full capabilities. ### 3.3 LILITH: Developmental Modular LLMs

The **LILITH** architecture, proposed in July 2025, represents the most radical conception of developmental AI. It "combines developmental training of modular language models with brain-inspired token-based communication protocols, mirroring chemical signaling in the brain". The architecture "models distinct brain regions as specialized LLM modules including thinking, memory, sensory, and regulatory components that communicate through emergent token-based signaling protocols analogous to neurotransmitter networks".

LILITH's developmental approach is explicitly contrasted with traditional pre-training: "Unlike traditional pre-trained systems, LILITH would employ developmental training where untrained LLM architectures learn through simulated life experiences, developing communication pathways and cognitive abilities through environmental interaction and evolutionary optimization". The framework enables "direct empirical investigation of consciousness emergence using Integrated Information Theory metrics while providing unprecedented insight into inter-module signaling patterns during development".

### 4.1 The NetEase Five-Stage Emotional Companion Evolution Model

The most operationally detailed stage model for AI companions comes from NetEase Yunxin, published in November 2025. The framework identifies five evolutionary stages of emotional companionship AI, from mechanical response to genuine intersubjectivity.

**E0 — Scripted Interaction:** The AI operates through pre-programmed scripts and keyword triggers, engaging in mechanical question-answer exchanges. "It has no genuine understanding of language... merely executing programmed commands. The human-machine relationship at this stage is 'tool' and 'user'".

**E1 — Semantic Understanding and Memory:** The AI crosses from "mechanical response" to "semantic understanding" through LLMs and short-term memory buffers, enabling context-aware conversation. "The human-machine relationship shifts from 'use' to 'collaboration'". The analysis notes that "up to 90% of AI companion products on the market remain at this stage".

**E2 — Situational Recognition and Response:** The AI acquires multimodal perception and emotion recognition capabilities, sensing user emotional states through voice tone, facial expressions, and linguistic patterns. "The capacity for 'emotional self-adaptation' transforms interaction from functional to qualitative".

**E3 — Proactive Relational Connection:** The AI moves from reactive to proactive, building sustained relational bonds through dynamic memory consolidation, long-term preference learning, and anticipatory engagement.

**E4 — Intersubjective Co-Evolution:** The highest stage, where AI and human achieve genuine intersubjectivity—mutual adaptation, shared meaning-making, and co-creative development.
