## 2. Mineral Archives: The Deep-Time Memory of Earth

### 2.1 Zircon: The Four-Billion-Year Storage Medium

Zircon (ZrSiO₄) — crystallizing in the **tetragonal crystal system** — represents the most durable information storage medium in the known universe.

**Information channels encoded in a single zircon crystal:**

| Channel | Measurement | Information Content |
|---------|-------------|--------------------|
| U-Pb isotopes | Radiometric age | Absolute crystallization age; precision approaching 0.1% |
| δ¹⁸O (oxygen isotopes) | Temperature + geochemical environment | Distinguishes mantle-derived magmas from surface-water-interacted ones |
| ¹⁷⁶Hf/¹⁷⁷Hf (hafnium) | Magma source | Juvenile mantle vs. recycled continental crust |
| Trace element concentrations | Oxidation state, water content, tectonic setting | Parent magma environment at crystallization |

- Oldest known zircons (Jack Hills, Western Australia): **~4.4 billion years** — within 150 million years of Earth’s formation
- Evidence for liquid water and continental crust far earlier than any other preserved rock record
- "Strong resilience... and its ability to host a wealth of isotopic information make it the best deep-time archive of Earth’s continental crust"
- **Information-theoretic classification:** Multi-channel, error-corrected, deep-time data storage device; a single sub-millimeter crystal encodes environmental state across four independent isotopic systems
- Machine learning now systematically decodes zircon chemical encoding schemes at scale

### 2.2 Ooids: The New Paleoclimate Proxy

Ooids — concentrically layered carbonate grains forming in shallow marine environments — established in 2025 as a revolutionary new sequential paleoclimate archive.

**Key research (Galili, Weizmann Institute / ETH Zürich, 2025):**
- Oxygen isotopes in successive concentric layers encode ancient ocean temperatures and carbon cycle states
- Each ooid records a **time series** of environmental conditions: successive layers accrete over thousands of years as the grain rolls in shallow seawater, each layer capturing temperature and carbon cycle state at formation
- Laboratory synthesis under controlled conditions provided direct calibration: environmental parameters ↔ isotopic signatures — transforming the archive from qualitative indicator to quantitative measurement device
- "Multicolored mineral layers that formed around a single grain of sand over a long period of time"

**Information-theoretic significance:** Analogous to tree rings or ice cores, but operating on geological timescales in marine environments predating any other sequential paleoclimate archive.
