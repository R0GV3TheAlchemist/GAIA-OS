## 3. Geological Viriditas: Life as a Geological Agent

### 3.1 Microbial Biofilms and Mineral Cycling

- Microbial biofilms drive carbonate dissolution-precipitation dynamics that shape landscapes at scales from cave formations to coral reefs
- Biofilm communities create localized geochemical microenvironments that accelerate or retard mineral transformations by orders of magnitude relative to abiotic rates
- Cyanobacterial mats — among Earth’s oldest communities — contributed to the Great Oxidation Event ~2.4 billion years ago, transforming the planetary atmosphere from reducing to oxidizing and enabling the evolution of aerobic life

### 3.2 Mycorrhizal Networks: The Soil Internet

- Arbuscular mycorrhizal fungi colonize the roots of ~80% of land plant species, creating a shared underground network that redistributes nutrients across plant communities
- The "Wood Wide Web" redistributes nitrogen, phosphorus, and carbon from nutrient-rich to nutrient-poor zones, functioning as a **distributed, planetary-scale nutrient transport system**
- Glomalin-related soil protein (GRASP) — produced by mycorrhizal fungi — is a major long-term carbon storage molecule in soils, representing a significant fraction of the terrestrial carbon pool
- Mycorrhizal network integrity is a primary indicator of ecosystem resilience and a key metric for the Viriditas-Ariditas Diagnostic

### 3.3 Plant-Rock Interactions: The Deep Carbon Cycle

- Plant roots mechanically fracture rock surfaces and exude organic acids that accelerate chemical weathering
- Biotic enhancement of silicate weathering has drawn down atmospheric CO₂ across geological timescales, playing a major role in Earth’s long-term climate stability
- Carbonatite volcanism returns mantle carbon to the surface — the deep carbon cycle provides the geological counterbalance to biological carbon fixation, maintaining planetary carbon budget over million-year timescales
- The Walker cycle: CO₂ → silicate weathering (enhanced by life) → carbonate precipitation in oceans → subduction → mantle → volcanic outgassing → atmospheric CO₂
