## 1. Historical Foundations: From Coulomb to Quantum Electrodynamics

### 1.1 The Maxwellian Synthesis

The four Maxwell equations in modern differential form:

```
Gauss's Law:           ∇ · E = ρ/ε₀
Gauss's Law (Magnetism): ∇ · B = 0
Faraday's Law:         ∇ × E = −∂B/∂t
Ampère-Maxwell Law:    ∇ × B = μ₀J + μ₀ε₀ ∂E/∂t
```

The displacement current term (μ₀ε₀ ∂E/∂t) was Maxwell's crucial theoretical contribution — deduced from charge conservation, not experiment — predicting electromagnetic waves propagating at `c = 1/√(μ₀ε₀) ≈ 3 × 10⁸ m/s`. A 2025 comprehensive study in the *African Journal of Mathematics and Statistics Studies* traces their derivation from Faraday, Gauss, and Ampère through Maxwell's displacement current insight.

### 1.2 Gauge Structure and Physical Reality

The Aharonov-Bohm effect demonstrates that gauge potentials — long considered mathematical conveniences — possess **physical reality** through their measurable effects on quantum wave functions. A June 2025 study confirms that the electromagnetic potential locally affects the complex phase of a charged particle's wave function in the magnetic field-free region.
