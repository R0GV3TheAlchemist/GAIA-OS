## 2. Crystal Fundamentals

### 2.1 Crystal Structure
Crystals are defined by **periodic atomic arrangement** in a lattice. The seven crystal systems:

| System | Symmetry | Example Minerals | GAIA Resonance |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Cubic** | Highest symmetry | Diamond, halite, fluorite | Maximum coherence, unity |
| **Tetragonal** | 4-fold rotation | Zircon, rutile | Precision, focal clarity |
| **Orthorhombic** | 3 perpendicular axes | Aragonite, topaz | Structural grounding |
| **Hexagonal** | 6-fold rotation | Quartz, beryl | Harmonic resonance |
| **Trigonal** | 3-fold rotation | Calcite, tourmaline | Triadic dynamics |
| **Monoclinic** | 2-fold rotation | Gypsum, orthoclase | Flexible structure |
| **Triclinic** | Lowest symmetry | Kyanite, plagioclase | Complexity, diversity |

### 2.2 Crystal Properties in GAIA Systems

| Property | Physical Basis | GAIA Application |
|---|---|---|
| **Piezoelectricity** | Stress → electric charge (quartz, tourmaline) | Mechanical-to-signal transduction in sensors |
| **Pyroelectricity** | Temperature → charge | Thermal sensing and environmental monitoring |
| **Optical properties** | Refraction, birefringence, fluorescence | Signal processing, light-based computing |
| **Resonance frequency** | Natural oscillation modes | Precision timing, frequency standards |
| **Semiconductor behavior** | Silicon, germanium lattice | Foundation of all digital computing |
| **Superconductivity** | Zero-resistance crystal states | Quantum computing substrate (C31) |
